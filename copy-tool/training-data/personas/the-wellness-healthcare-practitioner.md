## Psychographics: Who She Really Is

### Core Identity Traits
- **The Healer**: Her identity is built around caring for others — it's more than a job
- **The Ingredient Skeptic**: Reads every label; knows what parabens, phthalates, and endocrine disruptors do
- **The Exhausted Expert**: Knows exactly how stress affects the body — and feels it daily
- **The Walking Contradiction Avoider**: Can't recommend clean living while using toxic products

### Values
1. **Evidence and safety** — Won't use anything she can't stand behind
2. **Health as holistic** — What goes ON the body matters as much as what goes IN it
3. **Efficiency by necessity** — Zero time between patients for touch-ups
4. **Authenticity** — Practices what she preaches

### Worldview
She believes the body is a system, and everything matters — including what she puts on her skin. She's skeptical of marketing claims and does her own research. She's exhausted but won't compromise on quality or safety. She wants products that work with her body, not against it.

## The Long-Shift Reality

### Healthcare Worker Makeup Challenges
- **12-16 hour shifts** are common, with no time for touch-ups
- **Mask-wearing** requires transfer-proof products
- **Fluorescent lighting** shows every flaw
- **High-stress environment** means products that run with sweat or tears won't work

### What Works for Healthcare Workers
- **Less is more** — minimal product, maximum impact
- **Oil-free formulas** that won't slide under masks
- **Tubing mascara** that doesn't smudge
- **Setting spray** is essential, not optional
- **Cream formulas** over powder — won't cake under masks

### The Toxin Awareness Factor
As one nurse put it: "So many harmful chemicals in most products. As healthcare workers we are exposed to enough toxins in our jobs...we don't have to be exposed to more from our personal care products."

## Day in Her Life (Hospital Nurse Example)

**5:00 AM** — Alarm for 7am shift. Reluctant awakening.

**5:30 AM** — Coffee, quick breakfast. Mentally prepares for the day.

**6:00 AM** — Gets ready: scrubs, hair up, minimal makeup. Miracle Balm for color, mascara, done.

**6:30 AM** — Commute. Listens to podcast to decompress preemptively.

**7:00 AM - 7:00 PM** — The shift: patient rounds, charting, codes, family conversations, brief "lunch" standing up.

**3:00 PM** — Quick mirror check — somehow still presentable.

**7:30 PM** — Home. Shower. Finally exhale.

**8:00 PM** — Dinner, maybe some TV. Too tired to do much else.

**9:30 PM** — Skincare — the one ritual she won't skip. Clean products only.

**10:00 PM** — Sleep. Does it again tomorrow. Or at 7pm for night shift.
