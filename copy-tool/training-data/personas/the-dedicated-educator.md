# The Dedicated Educator

**Percentage of Customer Base:** ~15%

## Overview

Teachers, professors, and school administrators who spend their days in front of students and colleagues. They need to look presentable and approachable without looking "done." Practical spenders who value products that last and work effectively. They prioritize routines that are quick for early mornings and durable enough to last through a school day.

## Demographics

- **Profession:** K-12 Teachers, University Professors, School Administrators
- **Age Range:** 35-65
- **Socioeconomic Status:** Middle Class; educated and budget-conscious but willing to pay for quality
- **Family Status:** Often married, community-focused
- **Geography:** Suburban and rural towns across the US

## Key Motivations

1. **Durability** - Products that last all school day without touch-ups
2. **Value for money** - A little goes a long way
3. **Approachability** - Natural look that's professional but not distracting
4. **Quick application** - Early mornings demand efficiency

## Pain Points

- Limited time in the morning before school
- Can't touch up during the day (always in front of students)
- Want to look professional without looking overdone
- Tired by end of day, don't want makeup that shows fatigue
- Budget-conscious but frustrated by products that don't last

## Why Jones Road Appeals

"Educators need a look that is professional but not distracting. Jones Road offers a 'your skin but better' look that is perfect for the classroom. The longevity of the products and the fact that a little goes a long way appeals to their practical nature."

## Language That Resonates

- "Lasts from first bell to last"
- "Ready in 5 minutes"
- "Professional without the fuss"
- "Your skin but better"
- "One product, multiple uses"
- "A little goes a long way"

## Language to Avoid

- Anything suggesting high-maintenance routines
- Glamorous or dramatic looks
- Weekend/evening-specific messaging
- Luxury-focused language (focus on value instead)
