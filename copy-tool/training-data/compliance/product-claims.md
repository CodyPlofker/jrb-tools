## GENERAL COMPLIANCE RULES

1. **Never claim "long-wear" or "all-day wear"** unless the product's approved claims explicitly include it (currently only Gel Liner has this claim).

2. **Never claim specific percentages or clinical test results** unless they are listed in this document for that specific product.

3. **Never claim "non-comedogenic"** unless it's explicitly listed for that product.

4. **Never claim "dermatologist tested"** unless it's explicitly listed for that product.

5. **When making hydration/moisturization claims**, only use the exact language approved for each product. For example:
   - "24-hour hydration" can ONLY be used for Miracle Balm, WTF, and Just Enough Tinted Moisturizer
   - Do NOT extrapolate these claims to other products

6. **SPF Claims**: Only Everyday Sunscreen can make SPF-related claims. Never claim sun protection for any other product.

7. **When in doubt, use softer language** like "helps," "may improve," or "designed to" rather than absolute claims.

8. **Discontinued products**: Products marked as "to be discontinued" should not be heavily promoted in new marketing materials.
